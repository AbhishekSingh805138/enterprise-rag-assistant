Acme Corp Change Management Process
=====================================

1. OVERVIEW

All changes to production systems must follow this change management process.
A "change" is any modification to hardware, software, configuration, network,
or operational procedures in the production environment.

2. CHANGE CATEGORIES

Standard Changes:
  Pre-approved, low-risk changes that follow a documented procedure. Examples:
  routine patching, certificate renewals, DNS record updates. No CAB review
  required. Must be logged in the change management system (ServiceNow).

Normal Changes:
  Changes that require review and approval. Submitted at least 5 business days
  before the planned implementation date. Require a change request with:
  - Description and business justification
  - Risk assessment (impact and likelihood)
  - Implementation plan with step-by-step instructions
  - Rollback plan with estimated rollback time
  - Testing evidence (staging environment results)
  - Communication plan for affected users

Emergency Changes:
  Changes required to resolve a critical incident (SEV-1 or SEV-2). May bypass
  the standard approval process but require:
  - Verbal approval from the on-call Change Manager
  - Post-implementation documentation within 24 hours
  - Retrospective review at the next CAB meeting

3. CHANGE ADVISORY BOARD (CAB)

The CAB meets weekly on Wednesdays at 2 PM to review Normal change requests.
Membership includes:
  - VP of Engineering (Chair)
  - Lead SRE
  - Security representative
  - QA representative
  - Affected team leads (invited as needed)

Changes are approved, rejected, or deferred. Rejected changes receive written
feedback and may be resubmitted after addressing concerns.

4. MAINTENANCE WINDOWS

Standard maintenance window: Saturdays 2 AM - 6 AM ET.
Extended maintenance window (quarterly): First Saturday of each quarter, 12 AM - 8 AM ET.
Changes outside maintenance windows require VP of Engineering approval and must
include a customer notification plan.

5. CHANGE FREEZE

Change freezes are enforced during:
  - Last two weeks of each fiscal quarter (revenue-critical period)
  - Major product launches (declared by Product leadership)
  - Holiday seasons (December 20 - January 3)

Only emergency changes are permitted during change freeze periods.

6. POST-IMPLEMENTATION REVIEW

All Normal and Emergency changes must have a post-implementation review within
3 business days. The review assesses:
  - Was the change implemented as planned?
  - Were there unexpected issues or deviations?
  - Was the rollback plan adequate?
  - Are monitoring and alerts in place for the changed systems?
  - Were all stakeholders properly notified?

Metrics tracked: change success rate (target: >95%), mean time to implement,
number of emergency changes per month (target: <5% of total changes).
