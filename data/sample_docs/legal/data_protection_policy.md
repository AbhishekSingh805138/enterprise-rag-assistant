# Acme Corp Data Protection Policy

## Purpose
This policy establishes the framework for protecting personal data processed by
Acme Corp in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the
California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), and other applicable data protection laws.

## Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who
access, process, or store personal data on behalf of Acme Corp. It covers both
digital and physical records.

## Data Classification
Data is classified into four tiers:
- **Public** — Marketing materials, published reports. No restrictions on sharing.
- **Internal** — General business documents, meeting notes. Share within the company only.
- **Confidential** — Customer PII, financial records, HR data. Access restricted to
  authorized personnel. Must be encrypted at rest and in transit.
- **Restricted** — Trade secrets, M&A documents, board materials. Access requires
  explicit approval from the Chief Legal Officer. Must be stored in designated
  secure repositories only.

## Data Subject Rights
Acme Corp honors the following rights for data subjects:
1. **Right of Access** — Individuals may request a copy of their personal data.
   Responses must be provided within 30 days.
2. **Right to Rectification** — Inaccurate data must be corrected within 15 business days.
3. **Right to Erasure** — Requests for deletion must be honored unless a legal
   retention obligation applies. Processing time: 30 days.
4. **Right to Portability** — Data must be exportable in a machine-readable format (JSON or CSV).

## Breach Notification
In the event of a data breach, the Data Protection Officer (DPO) must be notified
within 4 hours of discovery. If the breach affects personal data of EU residents,
the relevant supervisory authority must be notified within 72 hours. Affected
individuals must be notified without undue delay if the breach poses a high risk
to their rights and freedoms.

## Data Processing Agreements
All third-party vendors who process personal data on behalf of Acme Corp must
sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) before receiving access. DPAs must include
clauses for data security standards, breach notification obligations, sub-processor
management, and audit rights. Vendors are reviewed annually for compliance.

## Retention and Disposal
Personal data must not be retained longer than necessary for its stated purpose.
Standard retention periods:
- Customer account data: 7 years after account closure
- Employee records: 3 years after separation
- Marketing consent records: 2 years after last interaction
- Financial transaction records: 10 years per regulatory requirement

Data disposal must use secure methods: digital data must be cryptographically
erased; physical records must be cross-cut shredded.
